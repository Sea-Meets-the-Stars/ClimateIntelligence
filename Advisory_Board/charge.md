# Charge of the Climate Intelligence Advisory Board

**Status:** DRAFT — 2026-07-22
**Effective date:** upon adoption
**Review:** annually (see Article X)

---

## Preamble

Climate Intelligence (CI) is a blog about the science of the climate, biodiversity,
and related topics, written in service of six guiding principles: that fact should
trump opinion while every measurement carries uncertainty and bias; that all
viewpoints are welcome but not accepted equally; that all life on Earth is relevant
and human life need not be prioritized; that religion and politics are left aside
wherever possible; that statistics are respected; and that mathematics trumps all.

The Advisory Board exists to hold CI to those principles. It is built, by design, to
**bracket the debate rather than to agree**: its founding members sit at opposite
poles of the informed conversation, and the Board's value lies in the honest tension
between credible viewpoints, not in consensus. This charter defines what the Board
does, how it is composed and governed, and the standing power it holds to speak to
the public when its advice is not followed.

## Article I — Mission

The Board advises CI on its coverage of the climate crisis and its solutions. It
aspires to represent a broad cross-section of the population of the United States,
while recognizing that CI hopes to inform readers beyond the U.S. as well. The Board
is **advisory**: it counsels CI leadership but does not direct it.

## Article II — Scope of advice

The Board advises on:

1. **Editorial balance and fairness** — whether CI honors Principles 1 and 2.
2. **Scientific accuracy** — whether CI's posts are factually and quantitatively sound.
3. **Purview and direction** — what CI chooses to cover, consistent with its stated
   purview.
4. **Tone and communication** — how CI presents its work.

The Board is **bound by** the six guiding principles and **may not amend them**. It
**may recommend** changes to the principles; leadership decides whether to adopt any
such recommendation.

## Article III — Composition and representation

1. The Board forms with two founding members: **Richard Nolthenius** and
   **Andrew Bojko**.
2. It is a **hybrid** body of subject-matter experts and engaged lay readers, with
   the two founders as the template for that mix.
3. The Board aims for a working size of **7–9 members** and shall not exceed **12**.
4. In its composition the Board **seeks breadth** — as a goal, not a quota — across
   **viewpoint, geography, age, demographics, and gender**. Breadth of *viewpoint*,
   spanning the range of credible positions on the science, is the Board's defining
   characteristic.
5. The Board is considered **non-functional if its membership falls below five**.

## Article IV — Membership

1. **Election.** The Board elects its own members by **simple majority (>50%) of the
   full current membership**. (While the Board has two members, seating a third
   therefore requires the agreement of both founders.)
2. **Terms.** Members serve **fixed three-year terms, renewable, with no term limit**.
   The two founders' initial terms are **staggered** — one two years, one three — so
   that the Board never turns over all at once.
3. **Vacancies.** A seat vacated by resignation, expiry, or removal is filled by the
   same simple-majority election. The Board is under **no obligation** to fill a
   vacant seat.
4. **Removal.** The Board may remove one of its own members by the **unanimous vote of
   its other members**.

## Article V — The Chair

1. The Board elects a **Chair** by simple majority for a **one-year term, renewable**.
2. The Chair's role is limited to **convening meetings and circulating the agenda**.
   The Chair holds **no powers beyond these** and casts **no tie-breaking vote** —
   a measure that does not reach a majority simply fails.

## Article VI — Meetings and operations

1. The Board meets **monthly**.
2. The Chair circulates an **agenda at least seven days before** each meeting and
   **shares it with CI leadership**.
3. **Quorum** is a **majority of current members**.
4. The Board renders its routine advice to CI leadership **by written report**.

## Article VII — Formal recommendations and the public-record power

1. A **formal recommendation** of the Board requires a **majority of the full current
   membership**.
2. **If CI leadership does not follow a formal recommendation**, the Board may
   **publish its findings and recommendation on the CI blog itself**.
3. Upon such publication, **CI leadership must acknowledge the recommendation and
   respond in writing within 30 days**, and that response is **appended to the
   published recommendation**.
4. A published recommendation states the **recommendation only** — not the vote tally
   and not individual dissents. There are no separate minority reports.

## Article VIII — Independence, conflicts, and confidentiality

1. **Volunteers.** Members serve without compensation.
2. **Conflicts of interest.** A member must **always disclose** a conflict — for
   example, when the Board weighs a CI post that characterizes that member's own work
   or advocacy — but, having disclosed, **may still vote**.
3. **Confidentiality (Chatham House Rule).** The Board's **deliberations are
   non-attributed**: what is said may be used but not attributed to any named member.
   A **published recommendation**, by contrast, is an **on-the-record document of the
   Board as a body**, naming no individual member unless that member opts in.

## Article IX — Relationship to CI leadership

1. "**CI leadership**" is presently the author and editor of Climate Intelligence
   (J. Xavier Prochaska); this may change.
2. Leadership **has no veto over the Board's membership**.
3. Leadership **may disband the entire Board**.

## Article X — Amending the charge; review

1. The Board may **amend this charge by unanimous vote**. Amendments are recorded and
   shared with CI leadership. *(Open item flagged in drafting: whether a charge
   amendment also requires leadership's written concurrence — see the Q&A record.)*
2. This charge and the six guiding principles are **distinct instruments**: the Board
   governs its own charter but may only *recommend* changes to the principles
   (Article II).
3. The Board **reviews this charge annually**. The charge has **no sunset**.
