## 1. International & assessment bodies

- **IPCC — Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change** — https://www.ipcc.ch/
  The UN body that synthesizes the literature into consensus assessments. AR6
  complete (WGI 2021, WGII/III 2022, Synthesis 2023); AR7 cycle underway (see
  §7). *The single most authoritative reference for consensus findings.*
- **WMO — State of the Global Climate** — https://wmo.int/publication-series/state-of-global-climate
  Authoritative annual global scorecard (temperature, GHGs, ocean heat, sea
  level, ice, extremes). Annual, each March. Latest: 2025 edition (Mar 2026).
- **WMO — Greenhouse Gas Bulletin** — https://wmo.int/publication-series/greenhouse-gas-bulletin
  Annual authoritative global CO₂/CH₄/N₂O concentrations. Latest: No. 21 (Oct 2025).
- **UNEP — Emissions Gap Report** — https://www.unep.org/resources/emissions-gap-report-2025
  Annual gap between pledges and Paris-aligned pathways. Latest: "Off Target" (Nov 2025).
- **UNEP — Adaptation Gap Report** — https://www.unep.org/resources/adaptation-gap-report-2025
  Companion annual on adaptation progress/finance. Latest: "Running on Empty" (Oct 2025).
- **UNFCCC** — https://unfccc.int/
  Treaty secretariat: COP process, Paris Agreement, NDC registry, Global Stocktake.
- **IEA — World Energy Outlook / Net Zero roadmap** — https://www.iea.org/ *(bot-protected)*
  The most-cited global energy scenarios (STEPS/APS/NZE); annual WEO (Oct) plus
  the annual Global Energy Review and Global EV Outlook.
- **IRENA** — https://www.irena.org/ *(bot-protected)*
  Renewables deployment and the definitive annual *Renewable Power Generation Costs*.
- **US Global Change Research Program / National Climate Assessment** — https://www.globalchange.gov/
  Legally mandated US regional impacts. ⚠️ **Unstable:** site taken offline
  mid-2025; cite NCA5 (2023) via NOAA mirrors if down. NCA6 in development.
- **EU Copernicus Climate Change Service (C3S)** — https://climate.copernicus.eu/
  ECMWF-run; ERA5 reanalysis, Climate Data Store, monthly/annual Global Climate
  Highlights. Best for near-real-time global temperature and open data.
- **Royal Society & US NAS — "Climate Change: Evidence and Causes"** — https://royalsociety.org/news-resources/projects/climate-change-evidence-causes/ *(bot-protected)*
  Plain-language Q&A from the two leading academies; best for myth-busting.

## 2. Research institutions & data centers

- **NASA GISS (GISTEMP)** — https://data.giss.nasa.gov/gistemp/ — global surface
  temperature record (led by Gavin Schmidt).
- **NOAA NCEI** — https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/ — US environmental data archive; monthly
  global analyses; hosts the BAMS annual *State of the Climate*.
- **Met Office Hadley Centre** — https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/ — HadCRUT5 and
  related observational records.
- **Berkeley Earth** — https://berkeleyearth.org/ — independent global temperature
  dataset and annual review; also air quality.
- **PIK — Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research** — https://www.pik-potsdam.de/en
  — Earth-system analysis, tipping points, transformation pathways.
- **Woodwell Climate Research Center** — https://www.woodwellclimate.org/ — carbon,
  forests, permafrost, physical climate risk.
- **CICERO (Oslo)** — https://cicero.oslo.no/en — atmospheric science, emissions
  metrics, climate policy/economics.
- **Global Carbon Project** — https://globalcarbonbudget.org/ — the annual Global
  Carbon Budget (see §3).

## 7. Recent developments snapshot (mid-2025 – mid-2026)

> Dated highlights from the research pass. Post-dates the Jan-2026 knowledge
> cutoff in places — **verify specific figures against the primary reports before
> publication.** Interpretive/contested items are flagged.

- **2025 = 2nd–3rd warmest year on record** (~1.43–1.47 °C above pre-industrial; datasets disagree on rank, effectively tied with 2023). [Copernicus, Jan 2026]
- **First 3-year period above 1.5 °C:** the 2023–2025 average exceeded 1.5 °C — a multi-year run, *not* a formal breach of the Paris long-term (~20-yr) goal. [Copernicus/Carbon Brief]
- **WMO State of the Global Climate 2025** (23 Mar 2026): among warmest on record; record/near-record ocean heat; Arctic sea ice near record low; added Earth's energy imbalance as a headline indicator.
- **Atmospheric CO₂ passed 430 ppm** at the May 2025 Mauna Loa peak (Scripps 430.2 / NOAA 430.5 ppm); June 2026 ≈ 431.4 ppm. [NOAA/Scripps]
- **Global Carbon Budget 2025** (13 Nov 2025): record fossil CO₂ ≈ **38.1 Gt** (+1.1%); remaining 1.5 °C budget (~170 GtCO₂) ≈ four years of current emissions — "virtually exhausted."
- **UNEP Emissions Gap Report 2025 ("Off Target"):** full NDC implementation → ~2.3–2.5 °C; new pledges "barely moved the needle."
- **COP30, Belém, Brazil** (Nov 2025): "Belém Package" — tripled adaptation finance, $1.3 trn/yr roadmap, Tropical Forests Forever Fund — but **no agreed fossil-fuel phase-out language**; Brazil launched a voluntary roadmap outside the text.
- **Solar = largest source of energy-demand growth for the first time** (IEA Global Energy Review 2026): solar generation +~600 TWh in 2025.
- **EV sales topped 20 million (~1 in 4 new cars)** in 2025 (IEA Global EV Outlook 2026); China ≈55% share.
- **First climate tipping point declared crossed — warm-water coral reefs** (Global Tipping Points Report 2025, ~160 scientists). *Interpretive expert assessment, not undisputed observation — treat framing as strong-but-debated.*
- **AMOC collapse risk flagged earlier than prior estimates** by some 2025 modelling. *Single-study/contested — highly uncertain; not consensus.*
- **IPCC AR7 underway but timeline unresolved:** 664 authors selected (Aug 2025); reports expected ~2028–29; governments' repeated failure to agree a timeline called "unprecedented."
- **Warming likely to breach 1.5 °C (long-term mean) within ~a decade** [UNEP, Nov 2025] — reframing 1.5 °C as a near-certain temporary overshoot.
