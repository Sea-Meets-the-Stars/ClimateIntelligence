### The four comments, and how I weighed them (Principles 1, 2, 4, 6)

The prompt says "consider critically," so I treated this as a Principle-2
exercise: adopt what the evidence supports, decline what it doesn't, and stay out
of politics (Principle 4). Three of the four points were substantively right.

**(1) Sink failure / tipping points / CO₂ fertilization.** Verified via web
search: the 2023 land-sink collapse is well-documented — record Mauna Loa CO₂
growth (3.37 ppm, ~86% above 2022) against ~0.6% fossil-emission growth, weakest
land sink since 2003 (Ke et al. 2024, *Nat. Sci. Rev.*; GCB 2024). This is a
clean, citable demonstration of Richard's core point, so I adopted it into §3.5
and added a carbon-cycle-feedback/tipping caveat to §5 (with Armstrong McKay et
al. 2022). The specific "CO₂ fertilization at half its 1980 level" claim traces to
Wang et al. 2020 (*Science*), but there is a published rebuttal (Zhu et al. 2021)
calling it a satellite artifact — so per Principle 2 I flagged it as important but
unsettled rather than stating the magnitude as fact.

**(2) Methane slope.** Per the CLAUDE.md calculation rule, I wrote
`CI_Reports/methane_growth_rate.py` and differenced NOAA's deseasonalized series
rather than eyeballing. Result: 2020s ≈ 11.4 ppb/yr (fastest decade on record) vs
2.3 in the 2000s, with a 2021–22 record spike (~16.6) that has *eased* to ~6–8 in
2024–25. So Richard is right that recent growth far exceeds the 2000s, and my old
Figure 5 was masking it by plotting the raw monthly mean (whose 2025 seasonal dip
looked like flattening). I redrew Figure 5 to overlay NOAA's deseasonalized trend
(bold) with decade growth-rate annotations, and added a methane paragraph to
§3.5. I kept the honest nuance that the very tail has eased — mathematics trumps
all (Principle 6), even when it slightly complicates the reviewer's framing.

**(3) IPCC not the final word / under-predicts.** Adopted in §1 ("used, not
worshipped"): consensus lag, and the genuine under-prediction cases (Arctic sea

reality" — that's asymmetric and unsupported; IPCC has been close/occasionally
high on GMST. Balances the existing low-sensitivity heterodox voices (§4) with the
high-sensitivity critique (Hansen, context §13).

**(4) Atmosphere vs emissions; unreliable reporting.** Adopted the *science* in
§3.5 — the measured concentration integrates all sources/sinks, so the Keeling
curve is firmer than bottom-up inventories, which do carry real uncertainty. Did
