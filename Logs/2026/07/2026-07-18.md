### Narrative

The task: add a world map to the report's population section (now **§9**) showing
each country's total fertility rate (TFR), color-coded on a scale centered at the
**2.1 replacement level**, plus supporting text.

**Tooling constraint and workaround.** The `ocean14` env has pandas/matplotlib/
numpy but **no geopandas, plotly, kaleido, or requests**. Rather than install a
heavy GDAL stack, I built the choropleth in **matplotlib only**: I downloaded a
Natural Earth 1:110m country GeoJSON (`curl`) and drew each country's polygons as
matplotlib patches in plate-carrée, shaded by a `TwoSlopeNorm` diverging colormap
(`RdBu_r`) with `vcenter=2.1` so below-replacement is blue and above is red, with
2.1 marked on the colorbar.

**Data.** Per-country TFR from the **World Bank API**, indicator SP.DYN.TFRT.IN
(underlying source UN WPP 2024), years 2015–2023, taking each country's most
recent non-null year (2023 for most). Both files cached under `CI_Reports/data/`
(`wb_tfr_raw.json`, `world_countries_ne110m.geojson`). One data-quality fix: the
World Bank "country/all" response mixes in ~48 regional/income **aggregates**
(WLD, EUU, …) that were inflating the summary counts, so I added an explicit
aggregate-code exclusion set. Clean country-level result: **117 of 217 countries
(54%) are below replacement**, median TFR 1.92, ranging from South Korea **0.72**
(lowest national) to Somalia **6.13** — an ~8× spread.

**Calculation scripted per CLAUDE.md.** `CI_Reports/make_fertility_map.py`
produces Figure 19 and prints coverage/summary stats. The map matched 169 of 177
polygons; 7 small/disputed territories (Taiwan, Kosovo, W. Sahara, N. Cyprus,
Somaliland, Falklands, Fr. S. Antarctic Lands) are left grey — no World Bank
ISO-3 record — which the caption discloses. Verified the rendered PNG visually
(blue Americas/Europe/East Asia/Australia; red Sahel + Somalia + Afghanistan).
